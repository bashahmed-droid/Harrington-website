Our Mission

Harrington was founded to solve the operational complexity surrounding shareholder liquidity transactions by providing institutional coordination while regulated financial institutions perform regulated activities.

Harrington Equity Group is a capital markets coordination firm that helps public companies, shareholders, family offices, and corporate advisers navigate complex liquidity transactions.

Rather than requiring clients to manage multiple broker relationships, onboarding procedures, legal documentation, and transaction logistics, Harrington serves as a single trusted point of contact throughout the transaction lifecycle.

Scope of Engagement

What Harrington does — and what it doesn't

Harrington is not a broker-dealer. Every regulated activity is performed exclusively by licensed execution partners.

Harrington coordinates

  • Client onboarding & documentation
  • Compliance & legal opinion coordination
  • Transfer agent & shareholder support
  • Execution partner coordination
  • Deposit monitoring & settlement follow-up

Harrington does not

  • Provide investment advice
  • Recommend securities or transactions
  • Execute trades or hold securities
  • Hold client money or provide custody
  • Accept trade instructions

The Broader Platform

Harrington capital markets ecosystem

Harrington maintains two separate, independent relationships — not a sequential hand-off.

Harrington is the client-facing coordination layer. Execution and custody sit with regulated broker-dealers; principal capital solutions sit with Red Rock Alternative Fund — a separate, independent entity.

Regulated Execution Partners

Licensed broker-dealers providing execution, custody, settlement, and compliance.

Red Rock Alternative Fund

Principal capital solutions — direct purchases, stock loan financing, debt acquisition, structured opportunities.

The availability of any capital solution is subject to applicable law, transaction-specific considerations, and the independent acceptance of the relevant execution partner or principal investor.
